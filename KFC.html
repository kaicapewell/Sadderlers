KFC, short for Kentucky Fried Chicken, is a globally recognized fast-food chain specializing in fried chicken and a variety of other delicious menu offerings. Founded in 1952 by Colonel Harland Sanders, KFC has become one of the most prominent names in the fast-food industry. The brand's secret recipe of 11 herbs and spices, combined with its unique cooking method, imparts a distinct and flavorful taste to its chicken. KFC offers a range of meal options, including buckets of chicken, chicken sandwiches, wraps, salads, and sides such as mashed potatoes, coleslaw, and biscuits. The company is known for its iconic "finger-lickin' good" slogan, emphasizing the enjoyable and indulgent experience of their food. With its bold red and white branding, KFC restaurants provide a casual and inviting atmosphere for customers to enjoy their meals. KFC has expanded its presence globally, with thousands of locations in various countries, serving millions of customers who crave its signature crispy and flavorful chicken.
